Case Studies

IT - Marketing & Agency Industry

Situation: Allied Global Services was tasked with selecting the first employee to start a brand new department and new business line for our client. The candidate would need to be a full stack developer with both technical and creative expertise. As the first employee in the space in a new business line, a sense of leadership, self-motivation, and autonomy were intangible traits required since there were no internal guides, use cases, or best practices. This was a true entrepreneurial opportunity and needed the right person to proof the business model. 

Constraints: Soft skills were defined as more important than technical aptitude.  As such, our client required a personality assessment and would weigh it more heavily than previous work experience.  The industry niche prompts polarizing candidate opinions and for those with personal interests in misalignment, the market for interested candidates is reduced greatly.

Solution: Allied Global Services worked on the role exclusively.  Instead of saving the assessment for post interview, we flipped the recruiting process upside down.  We sent the assessment to as many candidates as we could and defined it as step one to the application process.  Only candidates with satisfactory results were considered for the vetting processes.

Results: More than 800 tests were sent over 10 business days, 230 were completed, 19 applicants were viable.  All 19 were then screened, evaluated and recruited for the position.  Results on all 19 were provided to the client and the top three candidates were selected for client facing interview.  Since the hire, the client has seen month-over-month revenue increases and will be leveraging the current success to fund additional members in Q1 2018.

IT - Non-Profit Custom Software Build 

Situation: Under executive stakeholder scrutiny because of a legacy system breach to critical applications yielding unknown degrees of customer data theft, Allied Global Services was selected to identify, recruit and integrate three SME level Technical Leads into a greenfield software development project across legacy, testing and new development individual contributor capacities.

Constraints: The degree of the breach was unknown and the location was rural Kansas.  The existing development and support teams had not previously been a part of modern development efforts and possessed only minimal exposure if any to agile methodology, object oriented programming, continuous integration, automated testing, etc.

Solution: Multi-year contract commitments were designed with flexibility to ensure top talent engagement; blended on/offsite (primarily onsite), flexible start/end times with adherence to core hours when in office between 10am-2pm, options for 40 and 40+ hour work weeks as project required, combinations of five-8’s, nine-9’s, and four-10’s, and paid holidays could all be leveraged if needed to meet possible candidate requirements.  Established nine interview times, interview participants/decision makers all confirmed in attendance and established discussion points prior to executing search under the expectation Allied Global Services would fill all spots with only candidates capable of meeting expectations and allowing client to select their favorites.

Results: One week with three dedicated tenured executive recruiters identified, recruited, and scheduled nine interviews. More than 1,750 candidates were evaluated, 145 screened, 25 advanced through multiple rounds of internal tech screens, culture fits, and commitment assessments, 12 selected for references and the top nine were scheduled with client interviews. 

Interviews went so well that four were selected instead of three and future consultants have been pipelined given the cost/benefit analysis of how much work our contractors have been able to both complete individually and enable the existing client team to achieve.  Contractors are still on project, breach has been contained, future risk mitigated, and then entire project portfolio is ahead of schedule and exceeding stakeholder expectations.
